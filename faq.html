What do different karats mean? +

What do the numbers 10k, 12k, 14k, 18k, and 24k mean to you? Probably not much until you realize what these numbers represent. The term karat, represented by the letter “K” refers to the purity measurement. There are 24 units in every gold alloy, meaning 24K is the purest form of gold. So the higher the number, the more genuine it is. But why is this important? This matters because the Karat also determines the durability. While 24K gold is the most expensive, it is also going to be softer and more malleable, making it less durable.
